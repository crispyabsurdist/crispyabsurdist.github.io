Navigating the Web Industry with undiagnosed ADHD: A Personal and Professional Odyssey

As a full-stack web developer, my journey in the digital realm spans over a decade, characterized by a commitment to excellence. Beginning my exploration in the field of design, I studied Graphic Design for three years at Jönköping Tekniska Högskola. This foundational education laid the groundwork for a seamless transition into the dynamic world of web development. Following my passion, I pursued further studies at Högskolan i Skövde, delving into the intricacies of web development. Armed with a comprehensive understanding of both design principles and technical skills, I embarked on a professional journey that has been both challenging and rewarding.

In this trajectory, I encountered a significant turning point at the age of 32 when I was diagnosed with ADHD. Navigating the web industry with this newfound awareness brought unique challenges, especially in an environment where neurodivergent experiences often face a lack of understanding.

Approaching my 35th year, I find myself in Jönköping, Sweden, surrounded by the warmth of a loving family—a wife and daughter who are central to my personal narrative. The intersection of personal milestones and professional challenges has shaped not just my career but also my advocacy for fostering understanding of neurodivergence in the workplace.

My curiosity extends beyond traditional learning methods, and I am constantly exploring GitHub repositories and tech blogs to stay updated with the latest trends and innovations in web development. This continuous quest for knowledge not only fuels my passion but also keeps me at the forefront of the ever-evolving tech landscape.
